Our History

THE COMPANY
BEHIND THE
CHEMISTRY

LaFrance Manufacturing has been formulating specialty products for metal casting operations since the 1950s. We were early pioneers in water-extendable emulsions to replace solvent-based die parting agents.

Today our formulations serve operations across the globe. We work with your team to identify the right product for optimum results.

The Frankote ceramic coating series came out of a recognized gap in the market: foundry operations needed ladle and furnace surface protection that could withstand sustained thermal cycling without degrading. Conventional coatings fell short. Our ceramic-based chemistry filled that gap.

Today, the same direct approach that characterized the company's founding guides how we work. When a customer calls with a casting challenge, they reach our technical people — not a distributor who will forward the inquiry.

// Company milestones

1950s
Founded in St. Louis LaFrance Manufacturing established to serve the growing metal casting industry in the Midwest and beyond.
1970s
Water-based lubricant development First commercially viable water-extendable die release lubricants developed in response to the oil crisis — an industry-wide shift LaFrance helped lead.
1980s
Frankote ceramic series launched Introduction of ceramic-based protective coatings for ladles and furnace surfaces — meeting a need conventional chemistry couldn't address.
2000s
International expansion Formulations reach casting operations worldwide. Direct customer relationships and technical support extended across North America, Europe, and Asia.
Today
Ceramic-based innovations continue New ceramic formulations for exotic aluminum alloys and high-heat producing dies added to the Franlube series — the latest development in an ongoing program.
