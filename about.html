Our History

THE COMPANY
BEHIND THE
CHEMISTRY

LaFrance Manufacturing was founded in St. Louis in the 1960s with a straightforward mission: develop the right specialty chemistry for the metal casting industry and stand behind it. Six decades later, that hasn't changed.

We were early pioneers in water-extendable synthetic lubricants. When the oil crisis of the late 1970s forced the die casting industry to reconsider its dependence on oil-based release agents, LaFrance was already working on commercially viable water-based formulations. We didn't follow the industry's direction — we helped set it.

The Frankote ceramic coating series came out of a recognized gap in the market: foundry operations needed ladle and furnace surface protection that could withstand sustained thermal cycling without degrading. Conventional coatings fell short. Our ceramic-based chemistry filled that gap and has been refined continuously since its introduction.

Today, the same direct approach that characterized the company's founding guides how we work. When a customer calls with a casting challenge, they reach our technical people — not a distributor who will forward the inquiry. We understand alloy chemistry, die geometry, and process conditions, and we match our formulations to all three.

// Company milestones

1960s
Founded in St. Louis LaFrance Manufacturing established to serve the growing metal casting industry in the Midwest and beyond.
1970s
Water-based lubricant development First commercially viable water-extendable die release lubricants developed in response to the oil crisis — an industry-wide shift LaFrance helped lead.
1980s
Frankote ceramic series launched Introduction of ceramic-based protective coatings for ladles and furnace surfaces — meeting a need conventional chemistry couldn't address.
2000s
International expansion Formulations reach casting operations worldwide. Direct customer relationships and technical support extended across North America, Europe, and Asia.
Today
Ceramic-based innovations continue New ceramic formulations for exotic aluminum alloys and high-heat producing dies added to the Franlube series — the latest development in an ongoing program.
